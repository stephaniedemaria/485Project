Twitter Analysis Results

Please click here to visit our Github Repo!

The results of our project include a sentiment analysis using Twitter API, graphs, and wordclouds to illustrate Trump's tweets. The video that we posted to Flipgrid explains these features in more detail and our source code is posted on our Github repo. To follow along with our code, you will need to satisfy the requirements that are outlined on our repository's requirements.txt file. This txt file lists all the additional items that need to be downloaded to follow along with our project. The readme file on our Github repo provides detailed instructions as to how to follow along with the project so please check that file out as well!

To the right, the results of our sentiment analysis are showcased. The percentage of positive and negative tweets are first displayed. Those are followed by a sample of the actual tweets catagorized by positive or negative. The query itself can be changed as well as the sample size.

The graph and wordcloud are featured below. Our graph shows a comparison of the amount of negative words Trump has tweeted as president vs all time. As shown below, his use of negative words as president is much less than his use of the words historically. The only outlier to this is the word "witch hunt" as the use of this word during his presidency is very similar to his all time use of the word.

Our wordcloud is featured to the right of the graph and displays his most frequently used words. As shown by the wordcloud his top words are actually positive. For example, his top words are "great", "amp", and "new". These words all express a positive sentiment and this finding aligns with our chart's finding of trump using less negative words since his presidency started.
